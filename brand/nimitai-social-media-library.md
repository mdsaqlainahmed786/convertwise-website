## POST 4 — Educational Framework Hook
**Topic:** 3 signals on a sales call that tell you if the deal is already dead

---

3 signals on a sales call that tell you if the deal is already dead.

(Most reps miss all three.)

---

**1. The rep's talk ratio is above 70%**

If your rep is speaking 70% of the time, they're pitching — not discovering. Discovery is how you find the real pain. If you're not finding pain, you're not selling. You're presenting. And presentations don't close deals.

---

**2. No next step was confirmed before the call ended**

"I'll follow up next week" is not a next step. A next step has a date, a time, an attendee, and a reason. If none of those four exist at the end of a call, that deal is in limbo — and limbo is where deals go to die quietly.

---

**3. The buyer said "interesting" more than once**

"Interesting" is the polite way a prospect says "I don't see how this solves my problem." One "interesting" might be genuine. Two in a single call and your messaging hasn't landed. The rep hasn't connected the product to a real pain yet.

---

None of these signals are impossible to catch in the moment. They're just invisible if you're not measuring them.

We track all three — automatically — in Nimitai after every call your team takes. No manual review needed.

## POST 7 — Tactical Value Hook
**Topic:** 5 things you should track on every sales call (that most founders never measure)

---

5 things you should track on every sales call that most founders never measure.

These aren't opinions. These are the signals that show up consistently in lost deals.

---

**1. Talk ratio**
Are your reps talking more than 65% of the time? If yes, they're pitching when they should be discovering. Discovery is how you find real buying intent. Pitching before you've found pain is noise.

**2. Question density**
How many open-ended questions did your rep ask — and at what point in the call? Great reps front-load questions. Average reps front-load features. The questions you ask in the first 10 minutes determine whether you understand the buyer at all.

**3. Next step confirmation rate**
What percentage of your calls end with a specific, dated, time-stamped next step? This single metric predicts pipeline velocity better than almost anything else. No confirmed next step = no deal progression. Full stop.

**4. Competitor mention tracking**
When a competitor is mentioned on a call, how does your rep respond? Do they pivot, freeze, or address it directly? Knowing which competitors come up most — and which reps handle it best — is pure coaching gold.

**5. Pricing hesitation signals**
The moment a buyer hears your price, watch their language shift. Phrases like "we'd need to think about that," "let me check with the team," or "that's interesting" are detectable patterns. Catching them early lets you address them before they become silent deal-killers.

---

Most founders are flying blind on all five.

Nimitai tracks all of them automatically after every call.

Which one would move your business the most if you had full visibility into it right now?
